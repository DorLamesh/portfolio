About

I've been doing infrastructure work for about six years. Before DevOps I studied computer science and built things on the side — the usual path. What changed is that I stopped finding the code interesting and started finding the system interesting: who runs it, why it's shaped the way it is, what it would look like if you started over.

At Inuitive I work on the pipeline that takes code from commit to silicon. It's slow, it's capital-intensive, and the cost of a mistake is measured in weeks, not deploys. That environment has taught me more about reliability than any book.

I believe in small teams, direct feedback, and writing down your reasoning — not just your conclusions. I'm always up for a conversation about systems, teams, or anything that makes you think differently.
